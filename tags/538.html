Is the media moving left or the GOP moving right?
at 5:55 PM on Oct 28, 2016   |   filed under politics, fivethirtyeight

This is the question suggested by a chart from my new FiveThirtyEight post, looking at newspaper endorsements over the last few decades:

DW-NOMINATE data suggest the congressional GOP at least has moved right, though what exactly "right" means in the current climate is up for debate.

In researching this piece I read some 30–40 newspaper endorsements, and kept an informal tally of common tropes. Nearly every endorsement called Trump racist/xenophobic, dishonest, and "unfit" for the presidency, while Clinton's recurring tropes were her email server, her status as the first woman to receive a major party nomination, and the nebulous criticism that she "is flawed" or "has flaws." This last one stuck out to me.

What makes white people tick?
at 9:23 PM on Sep 23, 2016   |   filed under politics, race, institutions, fivethirtyeight

My new piece on FiveThirtyEight asks this question. Voters of color, of course, have decided resoundingly against Trump.

It's not central to the piece, but this is a significant point:

Republican ideology seems to be aligned with the values taught in historically white churches: For instance, black and Hispanic members of interracial congregations hold the same individualistic social attitudes as white churchgoers, suggesting that they may adopt these conservative views after extended exposure to white church values.

The linked study is to me an elegant demonstration of institutional inertia: values/practices remain largely stable, despite personnel changes. Consider this in conjunction with the fact that most of America's interracial institutions are historically white — black churches, schools, and neighborhoods have not been integrated.
