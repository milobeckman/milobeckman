Milo is a brilliant, dedicated math educator with an incomparable academic career. The author of Math Without Numbers (2021), Milo is committed to making math and science concepts clear, accessible, and exciting for students of all ages.

Before graduating from Stuyvesant High School and entering Harvard at 15, Milo captained and taught the New York City math team for two years, presented his AP Statistics project on Good Morning America, published crossword puzzles in the New York Times, developed a popular iOS app for SAT vocab studying, and completed two years of graduate-level math courses at the NYU Courant Institute.

Milo has taught, tutored, and coached math on three continents, developing innovative custom curricula for organizations and private clients. His teaching style is friendly and conversational, focused on developing strong mathematical intuition, prioritizing concepts and comprehension over rote memorization. Milo offers bespoke academic tutoring services, math enrichment sessions, and project-based mentorships for middle school, high school, and college students.
