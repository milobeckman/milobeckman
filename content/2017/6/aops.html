Art of Problem Solving forums
at 3:10 PM on Jun 5, 2017   |   filed under math, from the archives

For much of high school, I was an avid member of the Art of Problem Solving forums, a community for posing and solving contest math problems. Many original problems can be found at my account page; here is one example (original here):

Segments PA and PB are tangent to circle ω at points A and B respectively. Let CD be any diameter of ω. Lines AC and BD intersect at M, and lines AD and BC intersect at N. Prove that PA = PB = PM = PN.
