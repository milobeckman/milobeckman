at 11:20 AM on Oct 27, 2016   |   filed under crosswords

New crossword up today on the New York Times, and it's a kooky one. Solve times coming in at nearly double the average Thursday speeds. Give it a try here (NYT login required).

From my constructor's notes on XWordInfo (spoilers ahead):

I wanted to make a puzzle where the solution involved leaving some squares blank. My original idea involved theme entries like "RUNNING ON [EMPTY]" and "[NOTHING] PERSONAL" where the Down answers only made sense if you left the bracketed words blank. But Will rejected a couple iterations of this ... So the obvious next step was a puzzle where the Down answers worked as valid entries with OR without the letter from the intersecting theme entry.
